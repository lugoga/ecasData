Preface

The research team was contracted to conduct research for to assess and evaluate accuracy of existing fisheries dependent data to better inform policy and management of small-scale fisheries resources. Fisheries Data Analysis and Reporting of the Kibiti, Mafia and Kilwa Seascape, 2022 is the first volume in this annual series, which is intended to provide the public with easily accessible of fish catch and fisheries related economies information about the seascape artisanal fishing activities and fishing related industries.

This year’s report covers the years 2010 to 2020 and provide descriptive statistics for the following categories; artisanal fisheries total landings; artisanal catch rates; artisanal fisheries catch weight, number of fishing caught, number of fishers, catch rates and price trends. The report also provides management highlights for each district that include summary of stock status, updates on catch rates; trend of total landings; and other management schemes in the area like existance of collaborative fisheries management areas.

Information in this report came from several sources. Artisanal landings, revenue, and prive data were primarily obtained from the Fisheries Statistics Division, Office of the Ministry of Livestock and Fisheries. Records from the ministry encompass catch records of analog data storage system called Catch Assessment Survey (CAS); electronic Catch Assement Survey (e-CAS) and frame surveys obtained from the Ministry of Livestock and Fisheries, Fisheries Division. We also obtained catch related data of the Mafia Island from the Mafia Island Marine Protected Areas. Spatial data that illustrate the locations of collaborative fisheries management areas (CFMA) were obtained from WWF-Tanzania. Critical coastal habitats like mangroves, seagrass and coral reefs spatial datasets were collected from the archives of the Institute of Marine Sciences.

The purpose of this assignment is to provide a resource that will help fisheries managers and decision makers in the coastal and marine waters of Tanzania to quickly identify potential and sustainable coastal areas for fisheries. The report was developed so that it can be easily used by anyone involved in fisheries and coastal resource management, regardless of the water body or project scope. This report explains:

  • The state of the e-CAS database
  • How the data included in the report was gathered and analysed
  • How the indicators were selected and parameters computed
  • How to use the report

The report also include case studies that illustrate some of the collaborative fisheries management areas that clearly indicate fishing good practises for sustainable resources use.
